

> Companies are writing "Technical Writer" in the title while describing a systems architect in the responsibilities.

**The Title Is Writer. The Job Is Architect.**

The Principal Technical Writer role at Crunchafi has 8–12 years of experience required, ownership of information architecture, documentation metrics, platform strategy, cross-functional influence, and a mandate to redesign how knowledge is structured across the product lifecycle.

That is not a writing job.

That is an architecture job with a writing prerequisite.

The market has not updated its vocabulary to match what it actually needs. So it keeps hiring writers and giving them architect-level problems — then wondering why documentation doesn't scale.

The gap is not talent. It is the mental model.

Until companies name the role correctly, they will keep hiring for craft and losing on structure.
