**The Knowledge Layer Has Been Misclassified**

Organizations are building what is functionally AI data infrastructure — structured, chunked, versioned, retrievable content designed to ground enterprise AI systems. The decisions being made about metadata, taxonomy, componentization, and provenance are infrastructure decisions. They determine what AI can and cannot answer across the enterprise.

But the function is sitting inside content strategy. The hiring language is documentation language. The org structure is a publishing org.

The result is predictable: the knowledge layer gets optimized for human readability and channel consistency. The AI readiness requirements get bolted on as a workstream rather than designed in as a foundation.

**The gap is not capability — it is classification. The work has outgrown the function it lives in.**

**What this signals across the market:**

Every enterprise building RAG systems, knowledge assistants, or AI-powered service tools is making the same architectural decisions — chunking strategies, metadata models, retrieval patterns, content governance. Most are making those decisions inside content teams, IT teams, or AI teams independently.

No one has declared ownership of the knowledge layer as enterprise infrastructure.

The organizations that do — and hire or elevate accordingly — will have AI systems that work. The ones that don't will keep wondering why their models hallucinate on their own data.
