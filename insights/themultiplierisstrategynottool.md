

**Companies Are Hiring for LLM Leverage Before They Know What They're Leveraging**

Palo Alto Networks isn't hiring a writer. They're hiring someone to turn existing collateral into a multiplying system using LLMs.

That framing — _collateral multiplier_ — is precise and revealing.

It means the asset base already exists. The problem is that it doesn't scale across audiences, formats, or coverage tiers without manual intervention at every step.

LLMs are being positioned here not as creators, but as amplifiers of an already-credentialed content library. The knowledge architect's job is to build the process that makes that amplification safe, auditable, and repeatable.

This is a different problem than "generate more content."

It's: _how do you extend institutional knowledge to surfaces it has never reached, without losing the quality standard that made it trustworthy?_

Most companies haven't answered that question. They're hiring into it — one role at a time — without owning it as a system design problem.
