
> Companies are not hiring editors to improve documentation. They are hiring editors to stop their AI from hallucinating.

**Content Quality Is Now an AI Performance Variable**

The LLM-Aware Technical Editor role does not exist to make documentation clearer for humans.

It exists because the AI is hallucinating — and the root cause is upstream, in the content.

Articles written for human comprehension are failing RAG retrieval. Atomic steps are missing. Decision trees aren't structured. Terminology isn't normalized. The model encounters ambiguity and fills it with invented answers.

The editor's job is to remove the conditions that cause hallucination.

This is a new discipline: writing for machines as a primary audience, with human readability as a constraint, not a goal.

Content quality is no longer a communication problem. It is an AI reliability problem. And companies are only discovering this after the AI fails.

The documentation team is now part of the AI stack. Most organizations don't know that yet.
