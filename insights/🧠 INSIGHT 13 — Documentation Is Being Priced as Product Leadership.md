**Documentation Is Being Priced as Product Leadership**

RevenueCat's Senior Documentation Manager role is compensated at $169,000 — more than many senior engineering roles at comparable-stage companies.

It is the first hire chartered to own end-to-end documentation. It reports into the product organization. It is explicitly asked to treat AI readability as a first-class concern alongside human readability.

That is not a documentation job. That is a product systems role that happens to be expressed through content.

The signal is in the pricing. When a company compensates a documentation hire at this level, they are acknowledging that documentation has been creating compounding drag — on adoption, on support, on engineering velocity, on AI performance.

The investment signals the size of the problem they've been ignoring.

The market is not suddenly valuing documentation. It is finally putting a price on the cost of not having it.
