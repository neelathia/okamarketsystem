

> Companies are deploying AI tools faster than they are building the knowledge structures that make those tools work.

**AI Adoption Has a Knowledge Debt Problem**

Every organization is running an AI deployment. Few are running an AI knowledge strategy.

The tools get bought. The automations get built. The pilots get launched.

But the knowledge layer — what the tool does, how to use it, when not to use it, how it connects to other systems — never gets structured. It lives in Slack threads, individual memory, and one-off documentation that no one governs.

The result is predictable: AI adoption stalls not because the technology fails, but because the organization can't operationalize what it knows about the technology.

The Knowledge Implementation Manager role is a signal that companies are starting to recognize this. But they are still hiring for it as a documentation problem.

It is not. It is an infrastructure problem.

The knowledge layer is what determines whether AI investment compounds or decays
