STRUCTURE

RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Own and evolve the company's AI & Automation Knowledge Ecosystem
- Build, maintain, and govern a living AI Knowledge Base and Use Case Repository
- Lead monthly/quarterly knowledge-sharing sessions and internal demos
- Design and deliver AI trainings and workshops for technical and non-technical teams
- Establish KM standards, taxonomy, and documentation practices for AI/automation content
- Act as bridge between business, product, and technical teams
- Oversee data ingestion and quality control for knowledge assets
- Design and manage classification and retrieval structures
- Monitor adoption, gather feedback, improve knowledge processes

REQUIREMENTS:
- Experience in KM, AI enablement, internal platforms, or documentation in AI-driven orgs
- Understanding of generative AI, LLMs, RAG architectures, and knowledge engineering
- Hands-on experience building/managing knowledge bases or AI enablement platforms
- Ability to translate complex AI concepts for technical and non-technical audiences
- Cross-functional experience with engineering, data, product, and business teams
- Strong facilitation and training skills

NICE TO HAVE:
- Not explicitly listed

---

FUNCTION (OKA INTENT)

DO:
Build and govern a structured, living knowledge ecosystem for AI tools, automations, and best practices across the organization.

FIX:
AI tools and automations are being adopted without a coherent knowledge layer — usage is fragmented, undocumented, and not transferable across teams.

CHECK:
Adoption rates, knowledge base usage, training effectiveness, feedback loops, content accuracy and relevance.

CONSTRAINT:
Must serve both technical and non-technical audiences. Knowledge must be structured for AI retrieval (RAG), not just human reading.

---

INTERPRETATION

CORE IDEA:
This role exists to make AI adoption stick — by building the knowledge infrastructure that connects AI capability to organizational behavior.

IMPLIED NEED:
The company has deployed AI and automation tools but lacks the connective tissue — structured knowledge — that makes them usable at scale. This role is the system architect of that connective tissue.

MATURITY LEVEL:
System

RELATED ROLES:
- LLM-Aware Technical Editor (retrieval-oriented content)
- Senior Documentation Manager (documentation as system)
- Principal Technical Writer (documentation strategy)

PATTERN:
Companies are now treating knowledge management as an AI infrastructure problem, not an HR or documentation function.

---

YOUR POSITION:
This is the role that proves knowledge architecture is not support work — it is the operating layer that determines whether AI investment delivers returns. The person in this seat doesn't document AI; they make AI usable. That distinction is the gap most orgs don't see until the system fails.
