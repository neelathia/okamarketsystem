STRUCTURE

RESPONSIBILITIES:

- Train authors and cross-functional teams on optimizing articles for RAG retrieval
- Transform ad hoc IT support content into structured, modular articles with atomic steps and decision trees
- Audit AI responses and tool calling for accuracy of retrieved information

- Author and train AI-based writing assistants for non-technical users

- Review chat logs, search queries, deflection metrics to identify content gaps
- Propose and prioritize content changes based on measurable impact

- Background editing technical documentation that feeds chatbots, virtual agents, or LLM-based tools
- Ownership of content schema, information architecture, or documentation standards in large enterprise
- Expert proficiency in writing documentation for internally developed search and chatbot tooling

NICE TO HAVE:

---

FUNCTION (OKA INTENT)

DO:
Restructure existing knowledge base content to be machine-readable and RAG-retrievable, and establish content standards where machines are the primary audience.

FIX:
Existing documentation was written for humans and fails RAG retrieval — causing hallucinations, missed steps, and inaccurate AI responses.

CHECK:
RAG retrieval accuracy, AI response quality, deflection metrics, chat log analysis, content gap resolution.

CONSTRAINT:
Must retrofit existing content at scale while also setting forward-looking standards. The machine audience is primary; human readability is secondary but not optional.

---

INTERPRETATION

CORE IDEA:
This role treats documentation as AI training data — and the editor's job is to make that data structurally sound enough that AI systems don't fail when they retrieve it.

IMPLIED NEED:
The company's AI tools are only as good as the content they retrieve. They are experiencing AI failure not as a model problem, but as a content problem — and this role exists to fix that upstream.

YOUR POSITION:
This role is the clearest signal yet that documentation quality is now an AI reliability problem. The editor isn't fixing bad writing — they are fixing AI failure modes. The content layer has become the machine's nervous system, and someone has to own its structural integrity.
