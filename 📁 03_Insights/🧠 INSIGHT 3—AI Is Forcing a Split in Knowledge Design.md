**One Knowledge System Can’t Serve Humans and AI the Same Way**

A consistent pattern across roles is the implicit assumption that the same knowledge artifacts can serve both humans and AI systems.

In practice, they cannot.

Humans operate with context — they infer, interpret, and fill gaps.
AI systems require explicit structure — dependencies, decision logic, validation conditions.

As AI becomes embedded in workflows, knowledge design will split into:
- human-facing representations (steps, summaries, visuals)
- system-facing representations (structured units, logic, relationships)

The organizations that recognize and design for this split early will have a significant advantage in scaling both human operations and AI systems.
