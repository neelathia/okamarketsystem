
The Crunchtime Knowledge Architect posting is a signal worth naming explicitly.

They are not hiring because they lack content.
They are hiring because their content doesn't work in their AI systems.

Ticket deflection is failing. Chatbot accuracy is low. Agent-assist is underperforming.
The root cause is almost never the AI model — it's the knowledge architecture underneath it.

Unstructured content, inconsistent taxonomy, no governance, and no machine-readability
means AI tools have nothing clean to retrieve, rank, or reason over.

This role exists to fix the foundation.

What the market is beginning to realize:
AI systems are only as good as the knowledge layer they run on.
The next competitive differentiator in support operations isn't a better AI —
it's a better-structured knowledge base.

The companies that will win are the ones that treat knowledge as infrastructure,
not documentation.

The gap isn't capability. It's ownership of the knowledge layer as a system.
(Same signal as the broader pattern — but now showing up inside AI product teams,
not just KM departments.)
